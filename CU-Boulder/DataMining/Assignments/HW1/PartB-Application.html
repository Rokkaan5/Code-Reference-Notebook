After some analysis of the available information in the two datasets I found. I decided to keep the overlapping information which was only the job titles, job descriptions, company names, location (which had varying depths of information), and company descriptions.

Unfortunately, only one of the datasets had any salary information, the other one had (multiple) columns for salary information but contained all NaN values. However, since salary information is still useful I decided to keep the salary information provided by the one dataset (and so when I concatenate the two data frames the salary column for information from the second dataset will just be filled with NaNs).

df1 = djp_df[['Title','JobDescription','Company','Location','AboutC','Salary']]
df2 = trulia_df[['Job Title','Job Description','Location','Company Name','Companydescription']]

As mentioned before, if there are columns with similar information but different column names, then the data frames won’t concatenate (in a useful way). So, for instance, “Title” and “Job Title” in the data job posts.csv and trulia.csv data respectively refer to the same type of information but don’t have the same column name.

df1 = df1.rename(columns = {'Title':'Job Title','JobDescription':'Job Description','Company':'Company Name','AboutC':'Company Description'})
